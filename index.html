What is GST Registration?

GST (Goods and Services Tax) registration is mandatory for businesses whose annual turnover exceeds the threshold limit (₹40 lakhs for goods, ₹20 lakhs for services). It is also required for interstate suppliers, e-commerce operators, and certain specific categories of businesses.

Once registered, you receive a unique GSTIN (GST Identification Number) which enables you to collect GST, claim input tax credit, and comply with the law. Operating without GST registration when required can attract heavy penalties.

Who Needs GST Registration?

  • Businesses with annual turnover exceeding ₹40 lakhs (goods) or ₹20 lakhs (services)
  • Interstate suppliers of goods or services
  • E-commerce operators and aggregators
  • Casual taxable persons and non-resident taxable persons
  • Agents of a supplier / Input service distributors
  • Persons supplying online information, database access, or retrieval services
  • Compulsory registration categories as notified by the government

Income Tax Return Filing

Filing Income Tax Return (ITR) is a legal obligation for individuals and entities whose income exceeds the basic exemption limit. It's also essential for claiming tax refunds, carrying forward losses, and establishing financial credibility for loans and visas.

At Web Trends, we make ITR filing simple, accurate, and stress-free. Our tax experts analyze your income sources, identify all eligible deductions under the old and new tax regimes, and ensure maximum tax savings while maintaining complete compliance.

Who Should File ITR?

  • Individuals with annual income above ₹2.5 lakhs (old regime) / ₹3 lakhs (new regime)
  • Companies and firms (mandatory, regardless of income)
  • Individuals claiming tax refunds
  • Individuals with foreign assets or income
  • Persons who have deposited ₹1 crore+ in current account
  • Persons with electricity expenditure exceeding ₹1 lakh
  • Persons with foreign travel expenditure exceeding ₹2 lakhs

ITR Forms We Handle

ITR-1 (Sahaj)

For salaried individuals with income up to ₹50 lakhs from salary, one house property, and other sources.

ITR-2

For individuals and HUFs with income from salary, house property, capital gains, and foreign assets.

ITR-3

For individuals and HUFs having income from business or profession.

ITR-4 (Sugam)

For presumptive taxation for businesses with turnover up to ₹2 crores and professionals up to ₹50 lakhs.

ITR-5

For firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs, and artificial juridical persons.

ITR-6

For companies other than companies claiming exemption under Section 11.

Complete Guide to GST Registration in India 2025

Everything you need to know about GST registration, eligibility, and process.

Home / Blog / GST Guide
December 15, 2024 8 min read Prabin Kumar Saha

Goods and Services Tax (GST) is India's biggest indirect tax reform, unifying multiple taxes into a single framework. If you're running a business, understanding GST registration is not optional — it's essential.

What is GST?

GST is a comprehensive, multi-stage, destination-based indirect tax levied on every value addition. It replaced multiple cascading taxes like VAT, Service Tax, Central Excise, and others. Implemented on July 1, 2017, GST has simplified the indirect tax structure in India.

Who Needs GST Registration?

GST registration is mandatory for:

  • Businesses with annual turnover exceeding ₹40 lakhs (goods) or ₹20 lakhs (services)
  • Interstate suppliers of goods or services
  • E-commerce operators and sellers on e-commerce platforms
  • Agents supplying goods or services on behalf of others
  • Casual taxable persons and non-resident taxable persons
  • Input service distributors
  • Persons supplying online information or database access services from outside India

Types of GST

India follows a dual GST structure with three components:

  • CGST (Central GST): Collected by the Central Government on intra-state supplies
  • SGST (State GST): Collected by State Governments on intra-state supplies
  • IGST (Integrated GST): Collected by Central Government on inter-state supplies

Documents Required for GST Registration

  • PAN Card of the business or applicant
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Business address proof (Electricity bill, rent agreement, or ownership document)
  • Bank account details with cancelled cheque
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Authorization letter / Board resolution (for companies)
  • Partnership deed (for partnerships)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not registering when required — leads to heavy penalties
  • Providing incorrect business details in the application
  • Not filing GST returns on time — attracts late fees and interest
  • Not maintaining proper invoices and records
  • Choosing the wrong GST return form

How to File Income Tax Return: Step-by-Step

A simplified guide to filing your ITR accurately and on time.

Home / Blog / ITR Filing Guide
December 10, 2024 6 min read Prabin Kumar Saha

Filing your Income Tax Return (ITR) doesn't have to be complicated. With the right guidance, you can file accurately, claim maximum deductions, and avoid penalties.

What is ITR?

Income Tax Return is a form that taxpayers file to report their income, deductions, and tax liability to the Income Tax Department. Filing ITR is mandatory for individuals and entities above certain income thresholds.

Which ITR Form Should You Use?

  • ITR-1 (Sahaj): Salaried individuals with income up to ₹50 lakhs
  • ITR-2: Individuals with capital gains, foreign income, or multiple house properties
  • ITR-3: Individuals with business or professional income
  • ITR-4 (Sugam): Presumptive taxation for small businesses

Step-by-Step Filing Process

  1. Gather all documents — Form 16, bank statements, investment proofs, TDS certificates
  2. Choose the correct ITR form based on your income sources
  3. Register or log in to the Income Tax e-filing portal
  4. Fill in personal details, income details, and deductions
  5. Verify tax computation and pay any additional tax due
  6. E-verify your return using Aadhaar OTP, net banking, or DSC

Key Deductions Under Section 80C

  • PPF contributions — up to ₹1.5 lakhs
  • ELSS mutual funds — up to ₹1.5 lakhs
  • Life insurance premium — up to ₹1.5 lakhs
  • Home loan principal repayment — up to ₹1.5 lakhs
  • NPS contributions — up to ₹2 lakhs (with 80CCD)

Choosing the Right Business Structure in India

Compare different business structures and find the best fit for your goals.

Home / Blog / Business Registration
December 5, 2024 10 min read Prabin Kumar Saha

Choosing the right business structure is one of the most important decisions you'll make as an entrepreneur. It affects your liability, tax burden, compliance requirements, and ability to raise funds.

Sole Proprietorship

The simplest form of business. One person owns and operates the business with unlimited personal liability. Easy to start but difficult to scale. Best for freelancers and small local businesses.

  • Low cost and minimal compliance
  • Complete control with the owner
  • Unlimited personal liability
  • Difficult to raise capital

Partnership Firm

Two or more partners share profits, losses, and management. Governed by the Indian Partnership Act, 1932. Partners have unlimited liability jointly and severally.

LLP (Limited Liability Partnership)

Combines the flexibility of a partnership with limited liability protection. Partners are not personally liable for the LLP's debts. Ideal for professional services firms.

Private Limited Company

The most preferred structure for startups and growing businesses. Separate legal entity, limited liability, easy to raise funds, and strong credibility. Requires more compliance but offers the most benefits.

Before Filing/Submission
